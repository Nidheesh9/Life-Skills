## Question 1

**What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?**

**Answer**

* Focus fully on the speaker.
* Avoid distractions and own thoughts. 
* Take Notes.
* Don't interrupt.
* Respond only after the speaker finishes.
* Use door openers.
* Show active body language.
* Paraphrase and reflect.


## Question 2

**According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?**

**Answer**

Key points of Reflective Listening according to Fisher's model: 
* Listen actively without interrupting.
* Paraphrase the speaker's message.
* Reflect both words and emotions.
* Avoid giving immediate advice or solutions.

## Question 3

**What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

**Answer**

Some obstacles in my listening process are: 
* My own thoughts distract me while listening.
* Sometimes I don't focus properly.
* I don't take notes, so I later miss the key points.

## Question 4

**What can you do to improve your listening?**

**Answer**

I will work on my obstacles I identified. I will focus on what someone is saying, avoid distractions and take notes to remember key points.

## Question 5

**When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?**

**Answer**

I adopt a passive communication style when someone asks me to do something while I am already doing something else then I am unable to deny them.

## Question 6

**When do you switch into Aggressive communication style in your day to day life?**

**Answer**

I switch into an aggressive communication style when I am exhausted and busy doing something, especially when I see my friends or family is enjoying themselves or relaxing instead of helping me.

## Question 7

**When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day to day life?**

**Answer**

I adopt passive-aggressive communication when I dislike someone, and they ask me to do something. Instead of saying no directly, I delay it, refuse indirectly or respond with minimal effort. 

## Question 8

**How can you make your communication assertive?**

**Answer**

I can make my communication assertive by doing the following:
* Prioritize my own need and express them clearly.   
* Take care of others' feelings.
* Be confident and respectful.
* Say "no" politely when needed.
